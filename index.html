There are several reasons why synthetic fibers are developed, some of the main reasons include:

Cost-efficiency: Synthetic fibers are often cheaper to produce than natural fibers, as they do not require the same level of cultivation, harvesting, and processing.

Durability: Synthetic fibers tend to be more durable than natural fibers, as they are resistant to wear and tear, shrinking, and fading.

Versatility: Synthetic fibers can be engineered to have specific properties, such as stretch, wrinkle-resistance, and moisture-wicking, which makes them useful in a wide range of applications.

Consistency: Synthetic fibers are consistent in their properties, which allows for more accurate and reliable product development.

Sustainability: Synthetic fibers can be developed using recycled materials, which can help to reduce waste and minimize the environmental impact of textile production.

High-performance: Some synthetic fibers are developed for high-performance applications such as bullet-proof vests, aerospace, and medical implants.

High demand: The ever-growing population and increasing standard of living has led to high demand for clothing and household textiles, synthetic fibers are produced to meet this demand.

Diversification: Synthetic fibers offer a variety of textures and colors, which can be used to diversify the textile market and create new fashion trends.

The development of synthetic polymers was driven by several factors, like:

Cost-effectiveness: Synthetic polymers are typically less expensive to produce than natural polymers, such as rubber or cellulose, making them more accessible and affordable for consumers.

Versatility: Synthetic polymers can be engineered to have specific properties, such as strength, flexibility, or heat resistance, that make them suitable for a wide range of applications, from packaging to medical devices.

Sustainability: Synthetic polymers can be made from renewable resources, such as plant-based materials, and can have a lower environmental impact compared to traditional petroleum-based synthetic polymers.

Supply chain stability: Natural polymers, such as rubber and cellulose, can be subject to fluctuations in supply and price due to factors such as weather, disease, and geopolitical issues. Synthetic polymers provide a more stable and predictable source of raw materials for the production of a wide range of products.

In summary, the development of synthetic polymers has provided a cost-effective, versatile, customizable, and sustainable alternative to natural polymers, which has had a significant impact on many industries and has improved the quality of life for people around the world.

Interesting Facts

The Origin

Synthetics, or man-made fabrics, were first developed in America in the 1930s. The garment industry adopted their large-scale use during World War II when natural fibres were hard to obtain.

Future of Synthetic Fibers

Advances in technology have allowed for the development of new types of synthetic fibers that are more sustainable and environmentally friendly, such as recycled polyester and bio-based nylon.

Strength of Synthetic Fibers

Some synthetic fibers, like Kevlar, are incredibly strong and are used in products like bulletproof vests and body armor.
